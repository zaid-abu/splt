## Anti-patterns verdict

Splt does not look generically AI-generated at the product-thinking level. Its money-state copy, split preview, settlement direction, and friend prioritization are grounded in the actual job. The decorative layer is more formulaic: warm cream everywhere, repeated bordered cards, uppercase tracked section labels, routine staggered entrances, and a floating blurred pill tab bar with no labels.

The strongest visual tell is `src/app/(tabs)/_layout.tsx`: blur, inset floating placement, a wide shadow, icon-only navigation, and an active dot make a standard mobile control less legible. Removing roughly 25% of the decoration would make the good domain decisions feel more confident.

The deterministic web detector was intentionally skipped because the target is React Native and the detector is HTML/CSS-only. Source evidence found 137 `Pressable` tags, 39 explicit accessibility labels, only 2 accessibility-state declarations, 27 statically dark StatusBars, 19 spinner/indicator usages, 20 skeleton usages, 8 EmptyState usages, 5 RefreshControls, zero `isError` branches, and no used `ErrorState` component.

No reliable native browser overlay was available. A possible Metro listener existed on port 8081, but no inspectable rendered simulator/device surface could be reached, and DOM overlays cannot annotate native views.

## Overall impression

The app understands what users need to know, but it asks too much before routine actions and says too little after high-stakes ones. The biggest opportunity is to make the default expense path feel nearly automatic, then make every financial completion feel unmistakably saved, reversible, and safe.

## What is working

1. The dashboard leads with “what is my money situation?” rather than accounting data.
2. The live expense split preview and settlement direction sentence clearly show consequences before confirmation.
3. Friends “Needs attention,” useful empty states, skeletons, and destructive confirmations show thoughtful prioritization.

## Priority issues

### [P0] Account lifecycle breaks trust

Password reset points at a missing callback, email confirmation has no explicit state, Terms cannot be opened and are not truly required, and account deletion does not reliably remove the authentication identity.

Fix the complete lifecycle as explicit states: check email, resend, change email, callback validation, expired recovery link, recent authentication, deletion in progress, deletion complete, and support/recovery.

Suggested command: `$impeccable harden`

### [P1] Financial actions have no trustworthy ending

After save, the app usually navigates away. Users cannot distinguish saved, pending, duplicated, partially saved, or failed. Settlement can exceed the known debt without acknowledgement. There is no receipt, undo, reversal, or draft protection.

Make writes atomic/idempotent, show `Saving → Saved`, present a compact receipt with the resulting balance, offer reversal/undo where valid, warn about overpayment/currency mismatch, and preserve unfinished drafts.

Suggested command: `$impeccable harden`

### [P1] Navigation is attractive but unclear

Four icon-only tabs require recall. Stats and Profile files live inside the tab group without being declared tabs; Profile also has a root wrapper. Insights is conditionally discoverable only from the dashboard.

Use four labeled top-level destinations. Keep Profile in one place. Expose Insights consistently as a stable dashboard action or deliberate top-level destination. Mark non-tab routes explicitly hidden. Use standard platform navigation behavior.

Suggested command: `$impeccable clarify`

### [P1] Routine expense entry exposes advanced decisions too early

The first context-selection step is good. The second step exposes amount, title, date, currency, 9 categories, payer, three split methods, participant inclusion, and per-person values. A common “I paid for dinner, split equally” case looks almost as complex as an advanced split.

Default payer, date, category, currency, and equal split. Keep amount, description, context, and one confirmation visible. Collapse defaults into an editable summary; reveal people and custom/percentage controls only after “Adjust split” or “Split differently.”

Suggested command: `$impeccable distill`

### [P2] Accessibility and platform adaptation are incomplete

The global 1.3× font cap, low-contrast semantic colors, missing selected/busy states, undersized targets, hard-coded status bars, limited reduced-motion handling, portrait-only layout, disabled predictive Back, and phone-only navigation conflict with the calm product promise.

Remove the text cap, create accessible semantic text colors, expose selection/disabled/busy states, meet 44pt/48dp targets, centralize reduced-motion behavior, theme system chrome, and either implement expanded layouts or remove tablet support for v1.

Suggested command: `$impeccable adapt`

## Keep, add, simplify, remove

### Keep

- Human balance language and “Today’s money state.”
- Two-stage expense context selection.
- Live split preview and remaining-value indicators.
- Settlement direction and payer/recipient summary.
- Friends “Needs attention.”
- Skeletons, instructive empty states, sticky primary actions, and destructive confirmations.

### Add

- Saved/pending/offline/failed states and in-context retries.
- Financial receipts, resulting-balance preview, reversal/undo, and drafts.
- Labeled navigation and a stable Insights entry.
- First-run activation ending in a real task.
- Contextual help for split methods, currency conversion, and simplify debts.
- Search/More disclosure for long categories, currencies, icons, and participants.

### Simplify or move

- Collapse expense defaults into a single editable Details row.
- Move advanced splitting under “Adjust split.”
- Let Groups and Activity own their detail instead of repeating it on Dashboard.
- Move Tell a Friend outside account/security.
- Put invite education in empty and early group states.

### Remove

- Floating blurred pill navigation and wide decorative shadow.
- Routine per-screen stagger/spring entrances.
- Disabled Notifications switch.
- Generic “Error” titles with no recovery information.
- Press feedback on non-actions.
- Duplicate routes, component generations, and raw local color systems.

## Persona red flags

### First-timer

Icon-only tabs have no visible vocabulary. Onboarding explains features but does not coach a first success. Split methods lack concrete examples. “Remind” does something unexpected. Terms and recovery can dead-end before value.

### Frequent splitter

Every expense traverses the same full surface. There are no recent templates, remembered split rules, duplicate expense, recurring expenses, bulk settle, quick correction, or stable scoped Insights. Routine motion and haptics add latency.

### Money-sensitive user

“Someone is waiting on you” can feel shaming. Red/green text can fail contrast and depends on color. Conversion lacks visible source/rate time. Overpayment is not acknowledged. Save lacks a receipt. “Delete permanently” and “All caught up” promise more than the system supports.

## Minor observations

- Onboarding completion is device-global rather than account-scoped.
- “You pays” should be “You pay.”
- The Full settlement shortcut can display preferred currency while the group uses another currency.
- One notification mutation disables every request row.
- Expense notes appear in detail but cannot be entered during creation.
- Profile edits do not refresh AuthContext immediately.
- Search clear, FilterPill selected state, and primary disabled/busy state need accessibility metadata.
- Definite undersized targets include 36px dashboard actions and a 28px participant checkbox.
- Android predictive Back is disabled despite being a platform expectation.
