# Suma

## Register

product

## Platform

adaptive

## Users

Friends splitting trips and nights out are the primary audience. Roommates managing recurring household costs are the secondary audience. Both need to understand shared money quickly without maintaining a ledger by hand.

## Product Purpose

Suma keeps shared expenses, balances, and settlements understandable across friends and groups. Success means a person can see who needs attention, add an expense, and settle a balance without hunting through app sections.

## Positioning

Shared money is organized around the people and groups involved, not around accounting categories.

## Brand Personality

Clear, social, and composed. The product feels contemporary without becoming cold, financial, or overly playful.

## Anti-references

Avoid spreadsheet-like ledgers, persistent tab bars, ornamental glassmorphism, crypto aesthetics, and dashboards that turn every balance into a metric card.

## Design Principles

- People and groups are the navigation.
- Make the next useful action obvious in context.
- Explain balances in plain language before showing accounting detail.
- Use motion to preserve spatial context, not decorate transitions.
- Keep settlement flows calm, explicit, and reversible until confirmation.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Meet WCAG AA contrast, support reduced motion, never rely on color alone for balance status, use at least 44 pt iOS and 48 dp Android targets, and keep all core actions available without gestures.
